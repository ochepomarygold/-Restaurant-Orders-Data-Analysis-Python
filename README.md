# 🍽️  Restaurant-Orders-Data-Analysis-Python
Python-based restaurant sales analysis project focused on customer ordering behavior, menu performance, spending patterns, and peak business hours using transactional restaurant order data.

---
## Project Objective

The objective of this project is to analyze restaurant order data to uncover insights into customer preferences, high-performing menu items, spending behavior, and peak ordering periods.

This analysis helps support better decision-making in menu optimization, inventory planning, and restaurant operations.

---

## 📊 Business Questions Answered

1. Which menu items are ordered the most?
2. Which menu items are ordered the least?
3. Which food categories perform best overall?
4. Which orders generated the highest spending?
5. What are the peak restaurant ordering hours?
6. How does order volume vary throughout the day?
7. Which menu categories attract the highest customer demand?
8. What customer ordering trends can be identified from the data?

---

##  Key Insights

- Burgers and fries are among the most frequently ordered items, indicating strong customer preference for fast food options.
- Some menu items receive very low order counts and may require improvement or removal.
- Peak ordering hours occur between 12 PM and 2 PM, highlighting a strong lunch rush period.
- Asian cuisine emerged as one of the top-performing menu categories.
- Order patterns reveal clear customer preferences and demand trends across menu categories.

---

##  Recommendations

- Increase inventory availability for high-demand menu items during peak hours.
- Optimize staffing during lunch periods to improve service efficiency.
- Promote or redesign low-performing menu items to improve sales.
- Expand top-performing food categories and popular menu offerings.
- Use ordering trends to improve menu planning and operational forecasting.
